Solr Master

During my internship at FamilySearch, I spearheaded the Solr Master project, demonstrating my ability to address critical operational challenges. Notably, I successfully eliminated a 40-minute production outage associated with updates, significantly enhancing system reliability. To ensure robust performance, I designed and implemented comprehensive tests during the development stage, effectively catching multiple bugs and preventing potential issues in the live environment. Additionally, I played a crucial role in knowledge sharing by producing detailed documentation outlining the steps and processes implemented to mitigate downtime risks. This experience not only showcased my problem-solving skills but also highlighted my commitment to proactive and preventive measures within the development lifecycle.

Family Map App

As part of a class project, I developed the Family Map App, a comprehensive full-stack Android application created using Java and XML. In this project, I designed and implemented a backend server in Java, enabling the seamless reception of HTTP requests from clients. The key functionality of the app involved presenting users with a geographical view of their family history, providing an intuitive and visually engaging interface to explore and understand their genealogical connections. This hands-on experience not only enhanced my proficiency in mobile app development but also deepened my understanding of backend server architecture and its integration with frontend components.
